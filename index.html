Embryology

1. The zona reaction prevents:





Think about the reaction that ensures only one sperm fertilizes the egg.

2. At the time of ovulation, the oocyte is arrested in:





The secondary oocyte pauses just before finishing meiosis II.

3. Which germ layer forms the nervous system?





This is the outermost embryonic layer, also forming skin and hair.

4. Implantation normally occurs on which day after fertilization?





Happens a few days after the blastocyst reaches the uterus.

5. The primitive streak appears during which week of development?





This is when gastrulation starts—think “rule of 3” for development

6. Which of the following is not derived from the neural crest?





Neural crest forms peripheral nervous structures, pigment cells, and some endocrine tissue—not the light-sensitive layer of the eye.

7. Failure of closure of the anterior neuropore results in:





This defect is at the head end of the neural tube, not the spine.

8. Which of the following is the first event after sperm binds to the zona pellucida?





Enzymes are released from a special cap on the sperm before it enters.

9. During gastrulation, epiblast cells migrating through the primitive node form:





This midline rod-like structure induces neural tube formation.

10. In early embryonic development, the allantois in humans primarily contributes to:





In humans, this structure is small and vestigial but becomes part of a duct related to urine drainage6
